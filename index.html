Important Information

What is Asbestos & Why is it Dangerous?

Asbestos is a naturally occurring mineral that was widely used in UK construction materials from the 1950s through to 1999, when it was finally banned. It can be found in millions of homes, commercial buildings and industrial sites built or refurbished before the year 2000.

When asbestos-containing materials are disturbed or damaged, microscopic fibres are released into the air. These fibres are invisible to the naked eye, and when inhaled they can cause serious, life-threatening illnesses including:

  • Mesothelioma – a cancer of the lining of the lungs
  • Asbestosis – scarring of the lung tissue
  • Lung cancer caused by asbestos exposure
  • Pleural thickening affecting breathing
⚠️ Important: Asbestos is not dangerous if left undisturbed. However, if you suspect you have asbestos in your property, do not attempt to remove it yourself. Always use a licensed specialist like Yorkshire Asbestos Solutions.

Surveys

Types of Asbestos Survey We Offer

Not sure which survey you need? Our experts will advise you on the correct type for your situation.

📋 Management Survey

The standard survey required for the ongoing management of asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) in occupied premises. It is designed to locate ACMs that could be damaged or disturbed during normal activities.

Best for: Occupied buildings, landlords, and routine inspections.

🔨 Refurbishment & Demolition Survey

Required before any refurbishment or demolition work. This intrusive survey locates all ACMs in the area to be refurbished or demolished, even those in hidden locations.

Best for: Planned renovations, extensions, knock-throughs, and full demolitions.
