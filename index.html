What Is Asbestos and Why Is It Dangerous?

Asbestos is a naturally occurring mineral that was widely used in construction for its heat resistance and durability. However, it is now known that asbestos fibres, when inhaled, can cause serious health risks including lung cancer, asbestosis, and mesothelioma. Many asbestos-containing materials (ACMs) were used in buildings built before 1990, such as artex, floor tiles, and insulation.

Under the Control of Asbestos Regulations, there is a strict duty to manage asbestos in all non-domestic premises. The Health and Safety Executive (HSE) provides rigorous guidelines to ensure that any asbestos removal is carried out safely to prevent the release of dangerous fibres into the air.

If you suspect asbestos in your home or business, it is vital to contact asbestos removal specialists in leeds immediately. Attempting to remove asbestos yourself is extremely dangerous and illegal for many types of materials.

Do You Need an Asbestos Survey First?

Before any work begins, a qualified surveyor must assess the property. There are different asbestos survey types depending on your needs. A management survey is standard for ongoing safety, while a demolition survey is essential before structural changes.

If you suspect asbestos, do not disturb the material. Our asbestos removal in leeds team can visit your site, take samples, and provide a detailed report within 24-48 hours, giving you complete peace of mind.
